Research & Core Concepts

In computational robotics and swarm telemetry, defining formal bounds for agent configurations is critical for state estimation. A group of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) can efficiently obtain the information of an unknown target or location of interest. My current research trajectory focuses on evaluating these constraints to maximize information capture during localized search patterns.

Multi-Agent Target Localization. A state estimation framework wherein a coordinated cluster of autonomous nodes dynamically converges on the spatial coordinates of a moving target. In a heterogeneous sensing network, these UAVs utilize onboard sensors of varying types and qualities to gather noisy measurements of the target's states.
Information Maximization. The performance of an unbiased estimator can be quantified by the Cramer-Rao lower bound, which is the same as the inverse of Fisher information. Efficacy of localization gets enhanced if UAVs form an optimal formation geometry around the target, thus ensuring the maximization of the Fisher Information Matrix (FIM) of the unknown target.
